# Mary Lund Davis: Biography

*Extracted from the thesis "Mary Lund Davis: A Portrait in Three Histories" by Nevis Charles Granum (2023)*

---

## Early Life

**Born:** February 13, 1922, Sacramento, California
- Family legend says she was born in a canvas tent on a Sacramento rice field
- Lucky number: 13 (from her birthdate)

**Parents:**
- **Anders Lund** - Danish immigrant from Bredebro, Denmark
  - Came from a family of builders and sailors
  - Fled to Sacramento during WWI when ethnic Danes were crossing borders to fight for Germany
  - Taught Mary to sail, shoot a .22 rifle, and build houses
  - "Viking-like" - full of adventure and pioneering vigor
  
- **Freida Lund** - Danish immigrant, also from Bredebro
  - Came from a family of farmers
  - Never fully adjusted to life in the West
  - Never learned English

**Siblings:**
- Andrew (born 1925)
- Niels (born 1926)

**Childhood:**
- Grew up speaking Danish; learned English in kindergarten
- At age 8, began working alongside her father building houses during the Great Depression
- Father's touchstone phrase: "My father always told me I could do anything a man could do"

**Education:**
- C.K. McClatchy High School, Sacramento (graduated 1940)
  - Member of "Bridlespurs" horseback riding club
  - Member of "Pythagorean Sigma" math club
  - Member of "Palette and Brush" art club
- 1940: Designed and built family home with father in south Sacramento
  - Spanish Colonial style with Danish barn proportions
  - Scandinavian storm shutters

## University Years (1941-1945)

**University of Washington School of Architecture**
- Drove solo from Sacramento to Seattle to attend
- Studied under Lionel Pries
- Influenced by: Beaux Arts, Spanish/Mexican architecture, International School, Pacific Rim design

**Wartime Experience:**
- Each year, more male classmates left for WWII
- By senior year, only she and Pries remained
- Had extensive one-on-one time with her professor

**Classmates & Contemporaries:**
- Wendell Lovett (gave her his "Bikini Chair" as a gift)
- Ben McAdoo
- Alan Liddle (later designed Nevis's childhood bedroom)
- Dan Streisguth
- Robert Price

**Achievements:**
- Received Phi Beta Kappa honors
- Won student AIA gold medal (alongside George Davis)
- Interned with:
  - Moore and Massar
  - Chiarelli and Kirk
  - Thomas, Grainger, Thomas (Paul Hayden Kirk's firm)

**Graduated:** 1945
- First licensed female architect in Washington State after WWII

## Marriage & Business

**George Davis:**
- Met at University of Washington (classmates)
- Also won student AIA gold medal
- Mary made him wait 7 years

**Wedding:** May 26, 1950
- Held at Mary's family home in Sacramento
- Photos taken under the orange trees
- Drove away in their 1950 Ford convertible
- "They would never separate"

**Their shared vision:** "We were going to set the world on fire in architecture"

## Tacoma Mill Work Supply & Monitor Cabinets

**1948:** George purchased Tacoma Mill Work Supply from Uncle Tunis
- Price: $1 and a bottle of Jack Daniels whiskey

**Transformation:**
- Modernized the antiquated milling business
- Founded Monitor Cabinets within the business
- Influenced by Case Study designers:
  - Charles and Ray Eames
  - Pierre Koenig
  - Craig Ellwood
  - Eero Saarinen

**Innovation:**
- Early adopters of post-war pre-manufactured materials
- Laminate surfaces on MDO (medium-density overlay) plywood
- Rainbow spectrum of colorways
- Durable, cleanable cabinets with clean lines

**Markets:**
- Schools
- Hospitals
- Businesses
- Residential (custom and production)

**Mary's reflection:** "It influenced everything I did afterwards"

**Sold:** 1969 - Used proceeds to build Pampas Point

## Mary Lund Davis Architects

**Founded:** 1952
- Sole practitioner
- "A staggering accomplishment considering the patriarchal bent of the profession in the 1950s"

**Early Focus:**
- Kitchen and closet renovations
- Created profitable link between cabinetry business and architecture

**Major Works:**

### Del Monte (1954)
- First family home and architecture office
- "Skin and bones" tree house design
- Structural system designed to accept Tacoma Mill Work sheet goods
- **"She considered it her finest building"**
- Family sold it in 2011

### Alameda (1956)
- Second family home, down the road from Del Monte
- Light timber structure wrapped in plywood skin
- Built after daughter Kit was born
- Exhibited Pacific Northwest Modernism qualities:
  - Timber construction
  - Natural finishes
  - Indoor/outdoor living
  - Pacific Rim influences
  - Open interconnected spaces

### Fantastic Homes (1961)
- Partnership with Tacoma developer Ron Mitchell
- Association with Simpson Timber Company
- Over 200 affordable homes sold in first 7 months
- Prices: $3,995 - $7,995 (median home price was $14,700)
- Five models: 39er, 49er, 59er, 79er, Duplex
- Philosophy: "All low-cost housing does not have to be cheap"
- "We have proven that you can give good value, design, and livability in an inexpensive home"

### Recognition
- 1961: First AIA award for Tacoma Mill Work Supply office building
  - Signed by Paul Hayden Kirk
  - She cut it up to fit her presentation board: "Grandmother always served the present moment"
- 1964: Featured in Architectural Record's "Record Houses"
  - Only solo female practitioner honored
  - Alongside Richard Meier, I.M. Pei, Craig Ellwood

### Olson Residence (1965)
- Beach house for Mr. and Mrs. Garfield Olson (parents of architect Jim Olson)
- Located in Longbranch, WA, next to Paul Hayden Kirk's Dafoe House
- Striking reverse double A-frame timber design
- Gave design tips to young Jim Olson as they built projects together
- Jim remembered her smoking from long cigarette holders

### Pampas Point (1969)
- Final family home
- Named for pampas grass growing like a bowsprit over the bay
- Inspired by Frank Lloyd Wright: "The human spirit is best when not boxed in"
- Hexagonal house on 8' equilateral triangle grid
- No square corners
- Deep 8' covered overhangs for indoor/outdoor living
- 7' sliding glass doors throughout
- Monitor Cabinets and Japanese Shoji screens
- "The place our family calls home"

**Practice Closed:** 1970
- Not for lack of work, but by choice
- Shifted focus to landscape design
- "Designing a garden isn't all that different from designing a house, really"

## Sailing

Mary was a decorated sailboat skipper:
- Father taught her to sail on the Sacramento River
- Raced throughout the West Coast
- Won numerous "Adam's Cup" races
- Received "First Skipper" award for highest level of skill
- "She never removed her trophies from their plastic wrapping"

## Landscape Design & Garden

After closing her architecture practice, Mary focused on landscape design.

**Pampas Point Garden:**
- Started with overgrown 2-acre backyard of blackberry thickets
- An autumn flood washed everything away
- Found inspiration in the flowing patterns of rocks and debris
- "Nature stuck her foot in, so I said, 'Let's just join her'"

**Japanese Maple Collection:**
- Infamous for driving through neighborhoods seeking neglected Japanese maples
- Would pay homeowners and crane the trees onto flatbed trucks
- Collected 180 Japanese maples
- One was dated and cataloged by the Smithsonian Institute
  - Found by her son-in-law in Manchester, WA
  - The owner said it blocked his view
  - Possibly grew from seeds in a sailor's breast pocket

## Personal Characteristics

**Personality:**
- Confident and pioneering
- Action-oriented: "served the present moment"
- Witty and charming
- Independent spirit
- Avid photographer and archivist

**Habits & Preferences:**
- Smoked cigarettes from long cigarette holders
- Drank Grey Goose vodka from Marimekko teacups with one "rock"
- Photographed everything with disposable cameras
- Kept detailed scrapbooks and photo albums

**The Purple Horse:**
- Anniversary gift from George
- Life-sized fiberglass horse from a feed store in Haley, Idaho
- George craned it into their apple orchard as a surprise
- Mary spray-painted it purple
- "What shall we call it?" asked George
- "A horse of another color," Mary said

**Family Names:**
- Grandchildren called her "Amor"
- Her husband George also "passed" (brain cancer)
  - Ashes spread atop Glassford Peak in the Sawtooth Mountains

## Later Years

**After George's passing:**
- Moved to Palm Desert, California with her West Highland Terrier, Josh
- Purchased a William Cody house
- Painted all walls white except two in the hallway (lime green)
- Enjoyed John Wayne movies, Frank Sinatra, and crab cakes at "Shame on the Moon"

**Dementia:**
- Her memory declined in later years
- Her photography was an attempt to supplement or replace visual memory she was losing

**Passed:** 2008
- Ashes spread under a large maple tree in the upper field at Pampas Point

## Design Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Affordability with Quality**
   - "All low-cost housing does not have to be cheap"
   - Good design should be accessible

2. **Indoor/Outdoor Living**
   - Deep overhangs
   - Sliding glass doors
   - Connection to landscape

3. **Light and Air**
   - Clerestory windows
   - Natural ventilation
   - "Gathering" light

4. **Cabinetry as Architecture**
   - Used cabinets to divide and define space
   - Storage as functional walls
   - Monitor Cabinets in every project

5. **"Skin and Bones"**
   - Light timber structure
   - Thin, elegant exterior skin
   - Inspired by International Style + Pacific Northwest materials

6. **Serving the Present**
   - Practical, livable spaces
   - Design for current needs
   - "Grandmother always served the present moment"

### Influences

- **Frank Lloyd Wright:** "The human spirit is best when not boxed in"
- **Lionel Pries:** Pacific Rim influences, diverse creativity
- **Case Study Houses:** Pre-manufactured materials, industrialized processes
- **Danish Heritage:** Craftsmanship, simple forms, natural materials
